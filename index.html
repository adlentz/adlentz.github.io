About

Alyson is motivated by the belief that data and technology can be utilized more efficiently within healthcare to improve patient outcomes and overall quality of care. She would like to make an impact on improving the way data is accessed and used for research in healthcare, as well as at the point of care in order to make processes more efficient and accurate for both practitioners and patients.

As a recent graduate with a master’s degree in health informatics, she is looking to break into a data science/engineering career that has a positive impact on the healthcare industry. Within her master’s program, she has explored a variety of aspects of data science. These have included projects in machine learning, bioinformatics analysis, and mobile application development.

She has a wide range of work experience from customer service to wet lab work, as well as research and data engineering experience.

Feature Selection and Classification of CHF Patients

Comparison of feature selection and classification methods on an existing CHF dataset.

This project involved a comparative analysis of the feature selection and classification tools within python’s sklearn. This involved running KBest, RFE, and Chi2 feature selection on all of the features within an existing dataset. The outputs of these feature selection methods were analyzed and then the selected features were fed into a variety of classifiers to determine which classifier was the most efficient for classifying CHF from non-CHF patients based on the existing dataset.
